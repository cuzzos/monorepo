### The Opportunity
**Value Proposition:** AI Testing Copilot that makes iOS testing faster, smarter, and accessible to non-engineers through 4-phase evolution.

**4-Phase Evolution:**
- **Phase 1 (Months 1-6):** AI-enhanced error messages - debug 10x faster
- **Phase 2 (Months 7-12):** AI authoring assistant - write tests 5x faster
- **Phase 3 (Months 13-18):** Natural language authoring - non-engineers can test
- **Phase 4 (Months 19-24):** Multi-agent debugging - 90%+ diagnostic accuracy

**Core Differentiation:**
- ‚úÖ **Smart Test Selection** ‚≠êÔ∏è (Local + Cloud hybrid = 70-85% cost savings, impossible for competitors to copy)
- ‚úÖ Maestro foundation (fast, reliable execution - table stakes)
- ‚úÖ AI Copilot layer (intelligence that improves with usage)
- ‚úÖ ACE playbooks (self-improving, network effects)
- ‚úÖ Multi-agent consensus (UI + Logs + Network analysis)
- ‚úÖ Natural language testing (Phase 3+)

## üìñ Glossary of Terms & Acronyms

### Market & Business Terms

**TAM (Total Addressable Market)**
- The entire universe of potential customers if we had 100% market share
- Example: All 5 million iOS developers worldwide

**SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)**
- The portion of TAM we can realistically reach with our product
- Example: 500K iOS developers working on team projects (not hobbyists)

**SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**
- The market share we can realistically capture in near term (1-5 years)
- Example: 1% of SAM = 5,000 customers

**ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)**
- Total yearly revenue from subscriptions
- Example: 100 customers √ó $1,200/year = $120K ARR

**MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)**
- Total monthly revenue from subscriptions
- Example: 100 customers √ó $100/month = $10K MRR

**ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)**
- Average monthly revenue per customer
- Example: $50K MRR √∑ 500 customers = $100 ARPU

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**
- Total cost to acquire one customer (marketing + sales)
- Example: $10K ad spend √∑ 20 new customers = $500 CAC

**LTV (Lifetime Value)**
- Total revenue from a customer over their lifetime
- Example: $100 ARPU √ó 20 months average lifetime = $2,000 LTV

**Churn**
- Percentage of customers who cancel per month
- Example: 5 cancellations √∑ 100 customers = 5% monthly churn

**Gross Margin**
- Revenue minus direct costs (as percentage)
- Example: $100 revenue - $25 costs = 75% gross margin

**Burn Rate**
- Monthly cash spending (expenses minus revenue)
- Example: $50K expenses - $10K revenue = $40K/month burn rate

**Runway**
- Months until you run out of money
- Example: $200K in bank √∑ $40K burn = 5 months runway

**PMF (Product-Market Fit)**
- When your product strongly resonates with customers
- Signs: High retention, organic growth, customers would be "very disappointed" without it

### Technical Terms

**iOS Simulator**
- Software that mimics an iPhone/iPad on your Mac (for testing)
- Faster than real devices, but not 100% identical

**Maestro**
- Open-source tool for testing mobile apps using simple YAML files
- Example: `tapOn: "Login"` instead of complex code

**AI Vision / GPT-4 Vision**
- AI that can "see" and analyze images/screenshots
- Can detect layout bugs, wrong colors, visual issues

**Flakiness / Flaky Test**
- Test that sometimes passes, sometimes fails (without code changes)
- Example: Test fails 5% of the time due to timing issues

**False Positive**
- When AI incorrectly flags something as a bug (when it's actually fine)
- Example: AI says button is wrong color, but it's correct

**Unit Economics**
- Math of profit per customer (revenue minus cost)
- Example: $10 revenue/test - $1 cost/test = $9 profit/test

**Cache / Caching**
- Storing results to avoid repeating expensive operations
- Example: Don't re-analyze identical screenshots with AI

**API (Application Programming Interface)**
- Way for software to talk to other software
- Example: Our app calls OpenAI API to analyze screenshots

### Testing Terms

**XCTest / XCTest UI**
- Apple's official testing framework for iOS apps
- Known for being slow and flaky (hence our opportunity)

**Appium**
- Cross-platform testing tool (iOS + Android)
- More complex than Maestro but works everywhere

**CI/CD (Continuous Integration / Continuous Deployment)**
- Automatically testing and deploying code changes
- Example: GitHub Actions runs tests on every code commit

**Test Coverage**
- Percentage of code tested by automated tests
- Example: 80% test coverage = 80% of code is tested

**Regression**
- When a code change breaks something that previously worked
- Example: Update button code, now login flow breaks

**Smoke Test**
- Quick test of critical features (not exhaustive)
- Example: Can users login? Can they create account?

### Business Model Terms

**Freemium**
- Free basic version, paid premium features
- Example: 100 free tests/month, unlimited for $99/month

**PLG (Product-Led Growth)**
- Growth driven by product usage (not sales team)
- Example: Users try free tier, love it, upgrade to paid

**B2B (Business-to-Business)**
- Selling to companies (not individual consumers)
- Example: iOS testing platform for development teams

**SaaS (Software as a Service)**
- Subscription software accessed online/via app
- Example: Pay $99/month for cloud service vs buy software once

**Bootstrap**
- Building a company without outside investment (using own money)
- Opposite of raising VC funding

**Seed Round / Series A**
- Different stages of venture capital funding
- Seed: $1-5M early funding, Series A: $5-15M for growth

**Acqui-hire**
- Company acquisition primarily for the team, not the product
- Example: GitHub buys startup to hire the engineering team

**Strategic Acquisition**
- Company acquisition that fits buyer's strategy
- Example: GitHub buys iOS testing tool to add to their platform

**Exit**
- When founders sell company or take it public (IPO)
- Example: Sell to GitHub for $30M after 4 years

**Dilution**
- Ownership percentage decrease when raising investment
- Example: Raise $2M at $8M valuation = give up 20% ownership
